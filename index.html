History of Javascript

JavaScript was invented by Brendan Eich in 1995, and became an ECMA standard in 1997.
ECMAScript is the official name of the language.
ECMAScript versions have been abbreviated to ES1, ES2, ES3, ES5, and ES6.
Since 2016, versions are named by year (ECMAScript 2016, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020).

ES1

ES1, also known as ECMAScript 1, was the first standardized version of JavaScript.
It introduced fundamental features that laid the foundation for the language,
including: Variables (): ES1 introduced the keyword for variable declaration,
And making it possible to store and manipulate data within scripts.

ES2

ES2 was introduced in 1998
And there was a minor update that focused on improving the existing features and clarifying the language's specification.
No major new features were introduced in this version.

ES3

ES3 waas introduced in 1999
ES3 was a significant milestone in JavaScript's history,
solidifying its place as the language of the web.
Notable features added in ES3 included: Try-Catch: ES3 introduced structured error handling with the , , and statements.

ES4

The first draft of ECMAScript 4 was dated February 1999,
and over time many features were proposed, including classes, interfaces, and optional type annotations.
ES4 was beginning to look more like our modern-day version of TypeScript.

ES5

ES5 was officially released in December of 2009, and was known as a great “compromise.”
The 10 years of disagreement over ES4 was behind them, and this new version added several enhancements to the standard library.
ES5 became one of the most supported versions of JavaScript.

ES6

To address the limitations of the early versions of JavaScript,
the language was updated in 2015 with the release of ECMAScript 6 (ES6), also known as ES2015.
ES6 introduced a number of new features, including arrow functions, block-scoped variables, classes, and template literals.
